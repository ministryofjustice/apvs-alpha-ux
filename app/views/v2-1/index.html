Eligibility

You may be able to get help with the cost of visiting a close relative or partner in prison if you’re aged over 16, are on certain benefits or have a low income.

Relationship

You may be eligible if you are the prisoner’s:

Close relative

Partner

A partner means a person with whom the prisoner was living, as a couple, in an established relationship, immediately before the period of remand or imprisonment was imposed.

Any children living within the family unit are also eligible to receive assistance.

Escort to a qualifying child or young person

There are two types of escort to a prisoner’s child/children:

  • Carer
  • A person authorised to escort the child/children on someone’s behalf

1. Carer

If you are the guardian to the prisoner’s child/children, and you receive a qualifying low income benefit, you may be able to claim as an escort.

You will need to provide:

  • Birth Certificate for child/children being escorted
  • Proof of receipt of a qualifying benefit (e.g. Child Tax Credit award)

2. Authorised Person

If you are not a relative, partner or guardian, but have permission to take the prisoner’s child/children to make a visit, you may be able to claim as an escort.

You do not need to receive a qualifying low income benefit, but the child/children’s parent or guardian must receive a qualifying benefit for them.

You will need to provide:

  • A letter of permission from the child/children’s parent or guardian
  • Birth Certificate for child/children being escorted
  • Proof of receipt of a qualifying benefit (e.g. Child Tax Credit award)
Escort to a qualifying adult

Adult visitors may qualify for an escort.

The escort must complete an application form and wherever possible it should be sent to us at the same time as the visitors form.

A return journey between the home of the escort and qualifying visitor is payable subject to a maximum rate.

Where a car journey is being claimed, the total mileage allowance will normally be paid to the qualifying visitor unless advised otherwise. The volunteer driver rate only applies if the escort isn’t going into the visit.

Income/Benefits

You may be eligible if you have a relationship with the prisoner (as defined above), and if you (or someone on your behalf) are in receipt of one of the following:

Income Support, Jobseeker’s Allowance, Employment and Support Allowance

We confirm with the Department for Work and Pensions which benefit you receive and carry out regular checks.

Universal Credit (and you earn less than £1,250 a month)

An original award notice must be sent to us when you first claim and you must send us a new award notice every four months after that. An income limit applies.

Working Tax credits or Child tax credit with disability

To qualify you must be in receipt of one of the following:

  • Child Tax Credit
  • The disability element in Working Tax Credit
  • Working Tax Credit with Child Tax Credit

In each case the gross household income as shown on the tax credit award notice must not exceed a published limit.

The original award notice must be sent to us when first applying and thereafter when a when a new or amended award notice is issued.

Pension Credit

Recipients of Pension Credit are entitled to assisted prison visits subject to meeting the relationship criteria.

The original award notice must be sent to us when first applying and thereafter when a when a new or amended award notice is issued.

Health Certificate (HC2 or HC3)

The original certificate must be sent to us when first applying and thereafter when a when a new or amended certificate is issued.

Assisted prison visits may be backdated up to three months prior to the commencement of the health certificate, subject to meeting all other conditions.

The contribution shown on the HC3 certificate for travel to receive NHS treatment is deducted from the assisted prison visit payment. The application form for a HC certificate is available from APVU.

Start your application

Before you apply, you’ll need to have:

  • Your National Insurance number
  • Prisoner’s number and name of the Prison

Other ways to apply

You can apply by filling in a paper form instead.

You will need to complete the paper form and send it to the address on the form to get help with travel costs.
