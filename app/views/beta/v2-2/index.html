Get help with the cost of visiting someone in prison

You may get help with the cost of visiting someone in prison, if you’re a close relative, partner or are their only visitor in a month.

You must be over 16 and on a low income, or receiving a qualifying benefit.

Who can apply

You may be eligible if you receive:

  • Income Support
  • Income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) - Income related
  • Universal Credit
  • Working Tax Credit (with disability), Child Tax Credit or Pension Credit

or if you have an HC2 or HC3 Health Certificate.

More about who can apply

What you may be able to get help with

  • travel costs, For example, standard class rail tickets
  • overnight stays
  • other costs to do with your visit - For example, food, childminding, a volunteer driver or an escort if needed
More about what you can get help with

Start now

Before you start

You’ll need:

  • your National Insurance number
  • the name of the prison and the prisoner number of the person you’re visiting
  • details of your claim, For example, tickets

If you’ve used this service before you can return to a claim with your 8-digit reference number.

Other ways to apply

You can complete and send a paper application form to the Assisted Prison Visits Unit.
